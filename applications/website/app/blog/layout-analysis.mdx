---
title: "Why Generic Document AI Fails on Handwriting"
date: "May 7, 2026"
description: "What happened when we threw LayoutParser, OpenCV, and Tesseract at a page of handwritten notes."
---

I wanted to automate the layout segmentation of my handwritten notebook pages. 

Here is what failed and why:

- **LayoutParser (EfficientDet)**: Totally failed. It was trained on clean digital PDFs. The variance of my handwriting threw it off completely, and it misclassified the whole page as a single massive table.
- **Heuristic CV (Open OpenCV)**: Extremely brittle. Simple thresholding and Canny edge detection broke letters into tiny fragments or completely missed light pencil strokes under changing lighting.
- **Tesseract (Leptonica)**: My best classical run, but inconsistent handwriting line heights caused it to fragment single logical paragraphs into multiple disjointed lines.

These failures forced a pivot to a custom YOLOv11 Nano model. By labeling my own notes in Label Studio and exporting to ONNX, we can target edge inference on low-end hardware like an Intel Celeron, maintaining a robust offline layout AST.
